Steven Pinker's "Enlightenment Now" is a powerful defense of the values of the Enlightenment: reason, science, humanism, and progress. Pinker presents a wealth of data to argue that the world is getting better, not worse, thanks to these Enlightenment principles. He tackles common misconceptions and pessimism, providing a hopeful perspective on the future.

Reading Pinker’s book was a refreshing antidote to the often negative and pessimistic narratives that dominate the media. His data-driven approach and optimistic outlook are both inspiring and reassuring. "Enlightenment Now" made me appreciate the progress humanity has made and renewed my faith in the potential for continued improvement. Pinker's writing is engaging and accessible, making complex ideas digestible for a wide audience. This book is a must-read for anyone seeking a rational and optimistic view of human progress. It has reminded me of the importance of defending and promoting the values of reason, science, and humanism in our increasingly complex world.
