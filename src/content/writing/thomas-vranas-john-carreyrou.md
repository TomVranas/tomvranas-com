John Carreyrou’s "Bad Blood" is a gripping exposé of the Theranos scandal that reads like a high-stakes thriller. Carreyrou meticulously uncovers the web of deceit spun by Elizabeth Holmes and her associates, revealing the shocking lengths they went to in order to protect their fraudulent enterprise. This book is not just a tale of corporate malfeasance; it’s a sobering reminder of the dangers of unchecked ambition and the importance of ethical integrity in business.

As I read through Carreyrou's investigation, I found myself both fascinated and appalled by the sheer audacity of the Theranos deception. It’s a testament to Carreyrou's investigative prowess that he managed to piece together such a detailed account of the scandal. For anyone involved in the business world, this book is a stark warning about the perils of placing profit over principle. It made me reflect deeply on the ethical foundations of my own ventures and the importance of transparency and accountability. "Bad Blood" is a must-read for anyone who wants to understand the darker side of Silicon Valley's startup culture.
